## <a name="overview"></a>OVERVIEW & HOW TO USE THIS PACKAGE

### What is the AMC Clinical Examination?

The **AMC Clinical Examination** is an OSCE (Objective Structured Clinical Examination) required for international medical graduates seeking registration in Australia. It consists of **16 stations** (8 minutes each):

- **History-taking** (6-7 stations)
- **Physical examination** (4-5 stations)
- **Communication/Ethics** (2-3 stations)
- **Procedural skills** (1-2 stations)

### How These Materials Help You

This package provides **AMC Clinical-style notes** following the exact format and standards used in courses like Dr Aamir Saeed's preparation course, with:

âœ… **Exact phrases** examiners expect to hear
âœ… **Common IMG mistakes** to avoid
âœ… **8-minute station format** with realistic timing
âœ… **Role-play scripts** word-for-word
âœ… **Examiner marking criteria**

### How to Use These Materials

**Step 1: Read the Foundation Documents**
- Start with `/PROJECT_CONSTRAINTS.md` (project standards)
- Read this Master Index completely

**Step 2: Learn the Frameworks**
- **History-taking**: SOCRATES for pain, systematic approach for other complaints
- **Physical examination**: 5 Ps (Preparation, Position, Permission, Perform, Present)
- **Communication**: SPIKES for breaking bad news

**Step 3: Practice Each System**
- Work through history-taking notes for each system
- Practice physical examinations using the step-by-step guides
- Rehearse communication scripts OUT LOUD

**Step 4: Self-Assessment**
- Use mock OSCE stations (when available)
- Record yourself and watch back
- Time yourself strictly (8 minutes)

**Step 5: Daily Practice**
- **Target**: 2-3 history-taking scenarios daily
- **Target**: 1 physical examination daily
- **Target**: 1 communication scenario weekly

## <a name="quick-reference"></a>QUICK REFERENCE CARDS

### History-Taking Template (ANY Presentation)

**Opening** (30 sec):
- "Hello, I'm Dr [Name]. I understand you've come in today with [complaint]. I'd like to ask some questions to understand what's happening. Is that alright?"

**Focused History** (4 min):
- **Pain**: SOCRATES (Site, Onset, Character, Radiation, Associations, Time, Exacerbating/Relieving, Severity)
- **Other**: Onset, Duration, Severity, Progression, Timing, Triggers, Associated symptoms

**Systematic Questions** (2 min):
- Past medical history, Medications, Allergies, Family history, Social history

**Closure** (1 min):
- "Is there anything else you think I should know?"
- "Thank you. I'm going to examine you now / discuss this with my consultant."

**To Examiner** (1 min):
- Summarize history
- "My top 3 differentials are: [1], [2], [3]."
- "I would like to examine [system], check [investigations], and manage according to [guideline]."

### Physical Examination Template (ANY System)

**5 Ps Framework**:

1. **Preparation** (60 sec):
   - Wash hands
   - Introduction: "Hello, I'm Dr [Name]. I've been asked to examine your [system]. This will involve [explain]. Is that alright?"
   - Offer chaperone
   - Check for pain

2. **Position** (15 sec):
   - Appropriate for system (45Â° for CVS, sitting for Resp, lying for abdo)
   - Good lighting, adequate exposure

3. **Permission** (15 sec):
   - "I'm going to examine [areas]. Is that okay?"

4. **Perform** (5 min):
   - **Inspection** (end of bed, general, specific)
   - **Palpation**
   - **Percussion** (if applicable)
   - **Auscultation**
   - **Special tests**

5. **Present** (60 sec):
   - "On examination of [system]:"
   - Structured findings (General â†’ Specific)
   - Summary
   - "To complete examination, I would [DRE, fundoscopy, etc.]"

### Communication Station Template

**SPIKES for Breaking Bad News**:
- **S**etting: Private, sit down, tissues
- **P**erception: "What have you been told?"
- **I**nvitation: "Would you like me to explain?"
- **K**nowledge: Give info in chunks, pause
- **E**motions: Acknowledge, empathize
- **S**trategy: Make plan, safety-net, follow-up

**Key Phrases**:
- "I can see this is very difficult."
- "I'm sorry to have to tell you this."
- "It's completely understandable to feel [emotion]."
- "Take your time."

## <a name="common-mistakes"></a>COMMON IMG MISTAKES (Avoid These!)

### âŒ TOP 10 MISTAKES IN AMC CLINICAL

1. **âŒ Not introducing yourself or washing hands**
   - ALWAYS start every station: "Hello, I'm Dr [Name]." + Hand hygiene

2. **âŒ Using US terminology**
   - Say: paracetamol, GP, Emergency Department, salbutamol
   - NOT: acetaminophen, PCP, ER, albuterol

3. **âŒ Forgetting Australian spelling**
   - paediatric, anaemia, oestrogen, colour (NOT pediatric, anemia, estrogen, color)

4. **âŒ Not checking understanding**
   - ALWAYS say: "Does that make sense?" "Do you have any questions?"

5. **âŒ No safety-netting**
   - ALWAYS give specific red flags: "If you develop [X, Y, Z], call 000 or go to ED."

6. **âŒ Poor time management**
   - Stations are 8 minutes STRICT - practice with timer
   - Have a watch visible during exam

7. **âŒ Not offering chaperone**
   - For any intimate examination, ALWAYS offer chaperone

8. **âŒ Inadequate exposure for examination**
   - Can't examine through clothing
   - MUST expose adequately (while maintaining dignity)

9. **âŒ Talking to interpreter instead of patient**
   - ALWAYS look at patient, speak TO patient (not ABOUT patient to interpreter)

10. **âŒ No empathy in communication stations**
    - MUST show empathy: "I can see this is difficult." "I'm sorry you're going through this."

### Additional Mistakes by System

**History-Taking**:
- âŒ Not using SOCRATES for pain
- âŒ Forgetting to ask about red flags
- âŒ Not ranking differentials (must give top 3 in order)
- âŒ No mention of Australian guidelines (eTG, AMH)

**Physical Examination**:
- âŒ Not following 5 Ps structure
- âŒ Not saying findings out loud
- âŒ Forgetting to compare left vs right
- âŒ Not presenting systematically to examiner

**Communication**:
- âŒ Using medical jargon with patients
- âŒ Not responding to patient emotions
- âŒ Giving too much information at once (chunk information)
- âŒ Not summarizing at end

## <a name="assessment-criteria"></a>ASSESSMENT CRITERIA

### What AMC Clinical Examiners Look For

**Every station is marked on**:

1. **Communication Skills** (25%)
   - Introduction, consent, rapport
   - Clear explanations
   - Empathy, active listening
   - Checking understanding

2. **Clinical Knowledge** (35%)
   - Appropriate questions/examination
   - Accurate differentials
   - Safe management plan
   - Australian guidelines

3. **Clinical Reasoning** (25%)
   - Systematic approach
   - Prioritization (e.g., red flags first)
   - Integration of information
   - Risk assessment

4. **Professionalism** (15%)
   - Respectful manner
   - Privacy/dignity
   - Time management
   - Appropriate boundaries

### Passing Score

- **Pass**: Competent performance in all 4 domains
- **Borderline**: Minor deficiencies, but safe practice
- **Fail**: Major deficiencies, unsafe practice, or failed >4 stations

**You must pass OVERALL (not necessarily every station)**

### Automatic Fail Criteria

These will fail you immediately in a station:
- âŒ Major safety issue (e.g., prescribing dangerous medication, missing life-threatening diagnosis)
- âŒ Serious breach of ethics (e.g., breaking confidentiality, coercion)
- âŒ Aggressive or inappropriate behavior
- âŒ Not obtaining consent for intimate examination
