ðŸ“Š Detailed Module Breakdown

1. ENT Examination (Chapter 15)

Source: Talley & O'Connor Chapter 15

Key Components:

  • Otoscopy technique (external auditory canal, tympanic membrane)
  • Hearing tests: Rinne test (air vs bone conduction), Weber test (lateralization)
  • Nasal examination (turbinates, septum, polyps)
  • Oral cavity and throat (tonsils, pharynx, tongue)
  • Thyroid examination integrated
  • Neck lumps assessment (thyroglossal cyst, lymph nodes)

Special Features: 4 clinical findings tables, equipment checklist

2. Peripheral Vascular Examination (Chapter 8)

Source: Talley & O'Connor Chapter 8

Key Components:

  • Upper limb: hands, radial/brachial pulses, Allen's test, blood pressure
  • Lower limb: inspection, palpation, pulse examination (femoral to dorsalis pedis)
  • Buerger's test for arterial insufficiency
  • ABPI (Ankle-Brachial Pressure Index) interpretation
  • Auscultation for bruits (carotid, renal, femoral)
  • Ulcer differentiation (arterial vs venous vs neuropathic)
  • Acute limb ischaemia assessment (6 Ps)

Special Features: 6 Ps mnemonic, ABCDEF framework, ulcer comparison table

IMG Mistakes: 16 common errors including not comparing both sides, using thumb for pulses

3. Musculoskeletal Examination (Chapter 14)

Source: Talley & O'Connor Chapter 14

Key Components:

  • GALS Screening: Gait, Arms, Legs, Spine comprehensive screening
  • Hands/Wrists: RA vs OA signs, Phalen's, Tinel's, Finkelstein's tests
  • Elbows: Tennis elbow, golfer's elbow tests
  • Shoulders: Rotator cuff tests (Hawkins-Kennedy, Neer's, Jobe's), impingement
  • Spine: Cervical, thoracic, lumbar ROM, Schober's test, SLR, FABER
  • Hips: Thomas test, Trendelenburg test, leg length measurement
  • Knees: Effusion tests, ACL/PCL/MCL/LCL stability, meniscal tests
  • Feet/Ankles: Simmond's test, deformity identification

Special Features: Look-Feel-Move framework, 20+ special tests table, 10 mnemonics

IMG Mistakes: 16 errors including not asking about pain, inadequate exposure, rough handling

4. Thyroid Examination

Source: Talley & O'Connor (integrated from multiple chapters)

Key Components:

  • Inspection: Goitre, scars, asymmetry, swallowing test, tongue protrusion test
  • Palpation from behind: Australian standard technique, anatomical landmarks
  • Swallowing test: Water required, confirms thyroid mass
  • Pemberton's sign: Retrosternal goitre assessment
  • Eye examination (RELIEF mnemonic): Exophthalmos, lid lag, ophthalmoplegia for Graves'
  • Auscultation: Thyroid bruit (Graves' disease)
  • Functional assessment: Tremor, pulse, reflexes (hyper/hypothyroid signs)
  • Lymph node examination: Cancer screening

Special Features: Goitre grading (1-4), hyper/hypo comparison table, 6 mnemonics

IMG Mistakes: 8 errors including examining from front only, skipping eye exam

5. Lymph Node Examination

Source: Talley & O'Connor Chapter 7 (Haematology)

Key Components:

  • 7 S's Framework: Site, Size, Shape, Surface, Consistency, Surrounding tissues, Sensation
  • Cervical nodes: 9 groups (submental, submandibular, tonsillar, pre/post-auricular, occipital, anterior/posterior cervical, supraclavicular)
  • Axillary nodes: 5 groups (central, anterior, posterior, lateral, apical) with arm support technique
  • Epitrochlear nodes: Location, significance (bilateral = pathological)
  • Inguinal nodes: Horizontal/vertical groups, mandatory chaperone
  • Virchow's node: Left supraclavicular = abdominal malignancy (Troisier's sign)
  • Associated examination: Examine drainage area, spleen, liver

Special Features: Drainage area table, 7 S's detailed framework, systematic sequence mnemonic

IMG Mistakes: 12 errors including not examining from behind, skipping supraclavicular, not offering chaperone
