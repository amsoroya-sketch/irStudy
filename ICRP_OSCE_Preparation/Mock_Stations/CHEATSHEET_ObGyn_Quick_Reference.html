  1. üö®üö® SHOULDER TIP PAIN = PATHOGNOMONIC for haemoperitoneum (blood irritating diaphragm ‚Üí C3-C5 referred pain)

  2. üö®üö® ORTHOSTATIC SYMPTOMS = Feeling faint/dizzy on standing, near-syncope

  3. üö® UNILATERAL PELVIC PAIN = One-sided (right or left iliac fossa)

  1. üö® AGE ‚â•45 YEARS + Abnormal bleeding = MANDATORY endometrial sampling

  2. üö® INTERMENSTRUAL BLEEDING (IMB) = Bleeding between periods
    • Higher suspicion for endometrial polyps/cancer
  3. üö® POSTCOITAL BLEEDING (PCB) = Bleeding after sex
    • Consider cervical/endometrial pathology
  4. üö® SYMPTOMS OF ANAEMIA = Fatigue, dyspnoea, dizziness, pallor

    • Severe anaemia (Hb <70) may need transfusion

First Trimester Bleeding - Investigation Pathway:

ACUTE ASSESSMENT (Emergency Department):

  1. ABC + Vital signs: HR, BP, RR, temp (assess for shock)
  2. üö® IV access: TWO large-bore cannulae (14-16G) if unstable
  3. Bedside tests:
    • Urine Œ≤hCG (if not already confirmed positive)
    • Bedside transvaginal USS (if available, experienced operator)
  4. Laboratory (URGENT - priority 1):
    • üö® Serum Œ≤hCG (quantitative) - CRITICAL
    • FBC (Hb - may be normal initially)
    • Blood group & antibody screen (Rh status + Group & hold)
  5. Imaging:
    • üö® Formal transvaginal ultrasound (URGENT - within 1-2 hours)
    • Look for: IUP (confirms location), adnexal mass, free fluid (blood)
  6. üö® URGENT O&G REVIEW - call within 10-30 minutes if:
    • Shoulder tip pain
    • Orthostatic symptoms
    • Haemodynamic instability
    • Significant free fluid on scan

Heavy Menstrual Bleeding - Investigation Pathway:

ALL Women with HMB require:

  1. üö® FIRST-LINE (Mandatory):
    • FBC: Assess for iron-deficiency anaemia (low Hb, low MCV, low MCH)
    • üö® ENDOMETRIAL SAMPLING if age ‚â•45:
      • Pipelle biopsy (outpatient, 97.6% sensitive for cancer)
      • OR Hysteroscopy + D&C (gold standard, requires GA)
      • Purpose: Exclude endometrial hyperplasia/cancer
    • Pelvic ultrasound (transvaginal + transabdominal):
      • Assess for fibroids, adenomyosis, polyps, endometrial thickness
      • NOTE: USS does NOT replace endometrial sampling in women ‚â•45
  2. SECOND-LINE (If indicated):
    • Iron studies (if FBC shows anaemia) - confirm iron deficiency
    • Thyroid function tests (TSH, free T4) - hypothyroidism can cause HMB
    • Coagulation studies (if bleeding disorder suspected)
    • MRI pelvis (if USS equivocal OR planning surgery)

‚ùå TOP 10 IMG MISTAKES

1. ECTOPIC: Not Asking About Shoulder Tip Pain
Why fails: Shoulder tip pain is PATHOGNOMONIC for haemoperitoneum. Patients don't volunteer this (think they "slept funny"). YOU must ask.
‚úÖ CORRECT: In EVERY first trimester bleeding case, ask: "Have you had any pain in your shoulder or shoulder tip?"
2. ECTOPIC: Not Asking About Feeling Faint/Dizzy
Why fails: Orthostatic symptoms indicate hypovolemia from internal bleeding. Not asking = missing blood loss.
‚úÖ CORRECT: Ask: "Have you felt dizzy or lightheaded?" "Have you felt faint or like you might pass out?" "Have you actually fainted?"
3. ECTOPIC: Not Differentiating Unilateral vs Bilateral Pain
Why fails: UNILATERAL pain = ectopic (tubal). BILATERAL/central = miscarriage. Not asking = missing diagnostic clue.
‚úÖ CORRECT: Ask: "Is the pain mainly on one side, or is it in the middle/both sides?" ONE SIDE = ectopic most likely.
4. ECTOPIC: Not Recognizing URGENCY
Why fails: Shoulder pain + orthostatic symptoms = SURGICAL EMERGENCY. Treating as routine = patient could die.
‚úÖ CORRECT: Use urgent language: "This is a SURGICAL EMERGENCY requiring URGENT O&G review within 30 minutes." Call O&G NOW.
5. ECTOPIC: Not Asking About Blood Group (Rh Status)
Why fails: Rh NEGATIVE women need Anti-D within 72 hours. Failure to give = alloimmunisation = HDN in future pregnancies.
‚úÖ CORRECT: Ask: "Do you know your blood group?" If Rh NEGATIVE ‚Üí Anti-D 250 IU IM within 72 hrs.
6. HMB: Not Recognizing Age >45 = MANDATORY Endometrial Sampling
Why fails: RANZCOG 2024: ALL women ‚â•45 with abnormal bleeding MUST have endometrial sampling. Not recognizing = patient safety failure.
‚úÖ CORRECT: In ANY woman ‚â•45 with abnormal bleeding, investigation MUST include endometrial sampling (pipelle/hysteroscopy) to exclude cancer.
7. HMB: Not Quantifying Bleeding Severity
Why fails: "Heavy" is subjective. Need objective measures (pad count, clots, flooding) to assess severity.
‚úÖ CORRECT: Ask: (1) "How many pads/tampons per day?" (>8 = severe), (2) "Do you pass clots? How big?" (>2cm = abnormal), (3) "Do you flood through clothes?"
8. HMB: Not Asking About Intermenstrual/Postcoital Bleeding
Why fails: IMB and PCB are RED FLAGS for malignancy. Not asking = missing critical diagnostic clues.
‚úÖ CORRECT: ALWAYS ask: (1) "Do you have any bleeding between your periods?" (IMB), (2) "Do you have any bleeding after sex?" (PCB)
9. HMB: Not Assessing Quality of Life Impact
Why fails: HMB profoundly affects work, social life, emotional health. QOL impact guides treatment choice.
‚úÖ CORRECT: Ask: (1) "How is this affecting your work?" (2) "Impact on social life?" (3) "How do you feel emotionally?" Severe QOL impact ‚Üí surgical options.
10. HMB: Using "Wait and See" in Woman >45
Why fails: Cannot use "wait and see" in woman ‚â•45 with abnormal bleeding. MUST investigate to exclude malignancy.
‚úÖ CORRECT: In woman ‚â•45, plan MUST include: (1) FBC now, (2) Endometrial sampling within 2-4 weeks, (3) Pelvic USS. Do NOT suggest "wait and see."

üéØ Quick Exam Tips

First Trimester Bleeding Station:

  • Memorize the ECTOPIC RED FLAGS: Shoulder tip pain, orthostatic symptoms, unilateral pain, previous PID
  • Practice saying "Have you had any shoulder pain?" - make it automatic
  • Typical ectopic: LIGHT bleeding + ONE-SIDED pain + shoulder pain/dizziness
  • Know when to panic: Shoulder pain + orthostatic = call O&G NOW, get IV access, prepare for theatre
  • Don't forget Anti-D: If Rh negative ‚Üí 250 IU within 72 hours
  • Show empathy: Patient is losing first pregnancy and frightened. Acknowledge distress.

Heavy Menstrual Bleeding Station:

  • Memorize age cutoff: Age ‚â•45 + abnormal bleeding = ENDOMETRIAL SAMPLING mandatory
  • Use structured bleeding assessment: Frequency, Duration, Severity (QUANTIFY), IMB?, PCB?
  • Ask about the "3 Ps" for fibroids: Pressure (pelvic fullness), Pee (frequency), Poo (constipation)
  • Screen for anaemia EVERY TIME: Fatigue, dyspnoea, dizziness, pallor ‚Üí then FBC + iron
  • Assess QOL systematically: Work + Social + Emotional impact
  • Know management ladder: Tranexamic acid ‚Üí Mirena ‚Üí Ablation ‚Üí Hysterectomy
  • Practice investigation plan: "I would arrange: (1) FBC today, (2) Endometrial biopsy within 2 weeks - mandatory given her age, (3) Pelvic USS"
  • Australian terminology: "Heavy menstrual bleeding" (not DUB), "Pipelle biopsy," "Cervical screening test" (not Pap smear)
