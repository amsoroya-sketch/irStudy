HISTORY OF PRESENTING COMPLAINT

When asked about the pain, describe it as follows: Site:
  • "It was in the middle of my chest" (point to centre of sternum)

Onset:
  • "I was playing tennis with my mate - we'd been playing for about 30 minutes when it came on quite suddenly"
  • "I'd just served and felt this tightness in my chest"
  • Time: "About 2 hours ago, around 10am"

Character:
  • "It felt like a heavy weight on my chest, like someone was sitting on it"
  • "Tight, pressing feeling"
  • NOT sharp, NOT burning

Radiation:
  • "It spread to my left arm" (point to left upper arm)
  • "And a bit into my jaw" (only if specifically asked)

Associations:
  • "I felt a bit sweaty and clammy"
  • "A bit short of breath"
  • "Felt a bit sick in my stomach but didn't vomit"
  • NO palpitations, NO dizziness

Time course:
  • "It lasted about 15-20 minutes"
  • "It's mostly gone now but I still feel a dull ache"

Exacerbating factors:
  • "Definitely worse with exertion - I had to stop playing"

Relieving factors:
  • "When I sat down and rested, it gradually got better"
  • If asked: "My wife gave me one of my GTN sprays and that seemed to help a bit"

Severity:
  • "At its worst, I'd say 7 out of 10"
  • "Now it's about 2 out of 10"

PAST MEDICAL HISTORY

If asked:
  • "I have high blood pressure - I take tablets for that"
  • "And high cholesterol - also on tablets"
  • "I had angina diagnosed about 6 months ago after a stress test"
  • "I've never had a heart attack though"

MEDICATIONS

If asked:
  • "I take amlodipine for my blood pressure"
  • "Atorvastatin for cholesterol"
  • "GTN spray when I get chest pain - but I don't use it very often"
  • "And aspirin - a small dose every day"

ALLERGIES

If asked:
  • "No allergies to medications"

FAMILY HISTORY

If asked:
  • "My dad died of a heart attack when he was 63"
  • "My brother had a stent put in his heart last year - he's 62"

SOCIAL HISTORY

Smoking:
  • "I used to smoke for about 30 years - a pack a day"
  • "Quit 3 years ago"

Alcohol:
  • "Social drinker - maybe a couple of beers on the weekend"

Exercise:
  • "I play tennis twice a week usually"
  • "And walk the dog most days"

SYSTEMS REVIEW

If asked about other systems (only volunteer if specifically asked):
  • No cough
  • No leg swelling
  • No recent surgery or long flights
  • No indigestion problems

EMOTIONS & BEHAVIOR

Emotional State:
  • You're worried but trying to stay calm
  • You're particularly worried because of your family history
  • If doctor shows empathy, you open up more

If asked "What are you most worried about?":
  • "Well, with my dad and brother both having heart problems, I'm worried this could be a heart attack"

If doctor shows good communication:
  • Be more forthcoming with information
  • Thank them for their concern

If doctor is rushed or dismissive:
  • Become more anxious
  • May withhold information

QUESTIONS YOU SHOULD ASK (at appropriate time)

  • "Do you think it's my heart?"
  • "Do I need to be admitted?"
  • "Can I still play tennis?"

IDEAL APPROACH

Opening (30 sec):
  • Hand hygiene, introduction, consent
  • "Hello Mr Wilson, I'm Dr [Name]. I understand you've come in with chest pain today. I'd like to ask you some questions about this. Is that alright?"

SOCRATES History (3-4 min):
  • Systematically cover all 8 components
  • Identify üö® RED FLAGs: Exertional, central, tight, radiating to arm/jaw, associated sweating + SOB

Risk Factors (2 min):
  • Past history: Angina, hypertension, hypercholesterolaemia
  • Medications: Aspirin, statin, antihypertensive, GTN
  • Smoking: Ex-smoker (30 pack-years)
  • Family history: Strong (father died MI at 63, brother had stent)

Summary to Examiner (1 min):

"This is a 58-year-old man with exertional central chest pain, radiating to left arm and jaw, associated with sweating and shortness of breath, partially relieved by rest and GTN.

He has multiple cardiovascular risk factors including known angina, hypertension, hypercholesterolaemia, ex-smoker with 30 pack-year history, and strong family history of ischaemic heart disease.

My top 3 differentials are:
  1. Acute Coronary Syndrome (unstable angina or NSTEMI) - most likely given exertional pain, cardiac risk factors, radiation pattern
  2. Progression of stable angina - though concerning that pain lasted 15-20 min (longer than typical angina)
  3. Pulmonary embolism - less likely as no risk factors for DVT/PE, but pleuritic component possible

Immediate management:
  • URGENT ECG (within 10 minutes)
  • Serial troponins (0 and 3 hours)
  • Chest X-ray
  • Bloods: FBC, UEC, CRP
  • If troponin elevated: MONA (morphine if needed, oxygen if SpO2 <94%, nitrates, aspirin 300mg)
  • URGENT cardiology consult
  • Admit for observation

This patient should not be discharged from ED."
