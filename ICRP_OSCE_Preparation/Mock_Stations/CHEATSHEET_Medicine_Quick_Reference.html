M01: Shortness of Breath (Heart Failure vs COPD)

üéØ Key Learning Objectives:

  • Systematic dyspnoea assessment using "5 Ps" framework
  • Differentiate heart failure from COPD
  • Recognize "Magic Three" for heart failure: orthopnoea, PND, peripheral oedema
  • NYHA classification assessment

üìã "5 Ps" Framework for Dyspnoea:

  1. P1 - Precipitant: What triggers breathlessness?
  2. P2 - Progression: Getting better or worse? How quickly?
  3. P3 - Pattern: Constant vs intermittent? Time of day?
  4. P4 - Positional: Orthopnoea (lying flat)? PND (wakes gasping)?
  5. P5 - Previous: Any previous episodes? Known diagnoses?

üö® RED FLAGS - Heart Failure:

  • Orthopnoea: "How many pillows do you sleep on?" (HF if >2 pillows)
  • PND (Paroxysmal Nocturnal Dyspnoea): "Do you wake up gasping for breath at night?"
  • Peripheral oedema: "Any ankle swelling?" + recent weight gain
  • Frothy sputum: Pink, frothy = pulmonary oedema (not purulent = infection)

‚úÖ MUST ASK Questions:

  1. "How many pillows do you sleep on?" (orthopnoea)
  2. "Do you wake up at night short of breath?" (PND)
  3. "Have your ankles been swollen?" (peripheral oedema)
  4. "Have you gained weight recently?" (fluid retention)
  5. "What can you do now vs 6 months ago?" (functional capacity - NYHA)

M02: Dizziness & Syncope (Cardiac vs Neurological)

üìã "5 Ps" Framework for Syncope:

  1. P1 - Precipitant/Prodrome: Warning before collapse? Palpitations? Chest pain? Aura?
  2. P2 - Posture: Lying, sitting, or standing when collapsed?
  3. P3 - Phenomena During: What happened during collapse? Jerking? Tongue biting? Incontinence?
  4. P4 - Post-ictal: Immediate recovery or confused after? Headache?
  5. P5 - Previous Episodes: Ever happened before? How many times?

üö® RED FLAGS - Cardiac Syncope:

  • Exertional syncope: During exercise = cardiac until proven otherwise (FATAL)
  • No warning: Sudden collapse = cardiac arrhythmia
  • Palpitations before syncope: Arrhythmia likely
  • Chest pain before syncope: MI/PE/aortic dissection
  • Family history sudden death <40: HOCM, long QT syndrome

‚úÖ MUST ASK Questions:

  1. "Did you have any warning before you collapsed?"
  2. "What position were you in - lying, sitting, or standing?"
  3. "Did you feel your heart racing or skipping?"
  4. "What's the last thing you remember?"
  5. "Did anyone see you collapse? What did they say happened?"
  6. "Have any of your medications changed recently?" (beta-blockers!)
  7. "Do you drive?" (Austroads guidelines - must report syncope)

‚ö†Ô∏è Common IMG Mistakes:

  • ‚ùå Not asking about recent medication changes (beta-blocker increase = KEY in this station)
  • ‚ùå Not differentiating anoxic seizure (syncope with brief jerking) from epileptic seizure
  • ‚ùå Not assessing driving safety (Austroads guidelines = syncope = driving ban minimum 4 weeks)
  • ‚ùå Missing family history of sudden cardiac death

M03: Acute Confusion (Delirium Assessment)

üéØ Key Difference: COLLATERAL HISTORY (NOT direct patient interview)

This station tests your ability to take history from a RELATIVE/INFORMANT about a confused patient.

üìã CAM (Confusion Assessment Method) Criteria:

Delirium diagnosed if Features 1 + 2 + (3 OR 4):

  1. Acute onset + Fluctuating course: "When did the confusion start? Does it come and go?"
  2. Inattention: "Can she focus or is she easily distracted?"
  3. Disorganized thinking: "Is her conversation rambling or making sense?"
  4. Altered level of consciousness: "Is she drowsy, hyperactive, or normal?"

üìã PINCH ME Mnemonic (Delirium Causes):

  • P - Pain
  • I - Infection (UTI, pneumonia, cellulitis)
  • N - Nutrition (dehydration, thiamine deficiency)
  • C - Constipation
  • H - Hypoxia (respiratory failure, PE, cardiac failure)
  • M - Medications (anticholinergics, benzodiazepines, opioids)
  • E - Environment (hospital admission, sensory deprivation)

‚úÖ MUST ASK Questions (to relative/informant):

  1. "What was she like at BASELINE?" (memory, function, independence)
  2. "When did you first notice the confusion?"
  3. "Has it been getting worse, better, or fluctuating?"
  4. "Is she worse at any particular time of day?" (sundowning = worse at night)
  5. "Can she recognize you and family members?"
  6. "Has she been seeing or hearing things that aren't there?" (hallucinations)
  7. "Has she had any infections recently?" (UTI common trigger)
  8. "Any new medications or medication changes?"

‚ö†Ô∏è Common IMG Mistakes:

  • ‚ùå Not asking about BASELINE cognitive function - can't diagnose delirium if you don't know baseline!
  • ‚ùå Confusing delirium with dementia (see table below)
  • ‚ùå Not asking about sundowning (worse at night)
  • ‚ùå Recommending benzodiazepines (CONTRAINDICATED - worsens delirium)

üö® Classical Triad:

  • Polyuria: Excessive urination (>3L/day or >7 times/day)
  • Polydipsia: Excessive thirst and drinking
  • Weight loss: Unintentional (despite normal/increased appetite)
