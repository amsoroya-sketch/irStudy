## Available Textbooks

### Primary Resources (4 Major Textbooks - Split for Claude Code Access)

#### 1. **Talley & O'Connor's Clinical Examination** (8th Edition, 2017)
- **Location:** `Clinical_Examination_Split/` (23 parts, 1,135 pages)
- **Coverage:** Physical examination techniques, clinical findings, differential diagnosis
- **Best for:** Physical examination modules, systematic examination approaches
- **Australian:** ✅ YES (Australian authors, Australian medical practice)
- **AMC Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Essential - AMC OSCE gold standard)

#### 2. **Murtagh's General Practice** (8th Edition)
- **Location:** `Murtagh_General_Practice_Split/` (38 parts, 3,785 pages)
- **Coverage:** Primary care presentations, red flags, diagnostic approaches
- **Best for:** History taking, differential diagnosis, Australian GP context
- **Australian:** ✅ YES (Australian GP perspective, PBS/Medicare)
- **AMC Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Essential - covers 80% of AMC clinical scenarios)

#### 3. **AMC Anthology of Medical Conditions**
- **Location:** `AMC_Anthology_Split/` (5 parts, 483 pages)
- **Coverage:** AMC-specific clinical scenarios, OSCE station examples
- **Best for:** Mock OSCE stations, AMC exam format practice
- **Australian:** ✅ YES (AMC-specific content)
- **AMC Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Critical - official AMC resource)

#### 4. **Oxford Handbook of Emergency Medicine** (5th Edition, 2020)
- **Location:** `Oxford_Emergency_Medicine_Split/` (9 parts, 804 pages)
- **Coverage:** Emergency presentations, acute management protocols
- **Best for:** Emergency scenarios, resuscitation, acute medicine
- **Australian:** ⚠️ UK-based (adapt for Australian protocols - use eTG/ANZCOR)
- **AMC Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Very useful for emergency stations)

## How to Use This Cross-Reference System

### For Study Preparation

1. **Start with OSCE Module** (your HTML files)
   - Read the module thoroughly (60-90 minutes)
   - Identify any areas needing deeper understanding
   - Note the "Common IMG Mistakes" section

2. **Consult Primary Textbook** (as listed in table above)
   - Read the corresponding chapter/pages
   - Focus on clinical reasoning and examination technique
   - Compare OSCE module content with textbook depth

3. **Review Supplementary Resources** (for additional context)
   - Use for specific clarifications
   - Cross-reference differential diagnoses
   - Verify Australian protocols (if textbook is UK-based)

### For Specific Learning Needs

**Need:** Deeper understanding of differential diagnosis
→ **Resources:** Churchill's Pocketbook, Murtagh GP, AMC Anthology

**Need:** Physical examination technique practice
→ **Resources:** Talley & O'Connor (watch technique videos if available)

**Need:** Emergency management protocols
→ **Resources:** Oxford EM, ANZCOR guidelines (external), eTG (external)

**Need:** Australian-specific context
→ **Resources:** Murtagh GP, AMC Handbook, Antenatal Guidelines KEMH

**Need:** AMC OSCE format practice
→ **Resources:** AMC Anthology, AMC Handbook

## Textbook Access Strategy

### Option A: Direct PDF Access
All textbooks are available in `Books/Books for AMCClincal/` directory.

**Advantages:** Full access to all pages
**Disadvantages:** Large files (100 MB for Talley), harder to search

### Option B: Split PDF Access (Recommended for Claude Code)
Major textbooks split into 50-100 page chunks in respective directories:
- `Clinical_Examination_Split/` - 23 parts
- `Murtagh_General_Practice_Split/` - 38 parts
- `AMC_Anthology_Split/` - 5 parts
- `Oxford_Emergency_Medicine_Split/` - 9 parts

**Advantages:** Easier to reference specific pages, faster loading
**Disadvantages:** Need to know which part contains desired content

### Option C: Ask Claude Code to Extract Content
Use Claude Code to:
1. Read specific page ranges from split PDFs
2. Extract tables, algorithms, or clinical pearls
3. Summarize complex chapters
4. Create custom flashcards from textbook content

**Example prompts:**
- "Extract the causes of chest pain from Murtagh GP part 2, pages 120-135"
- "Summarize the cardiovascular examination technique from Talley Clinical Exam part 2"
- "Create a table comparing DKA vs HHS from AMC Anthology part 1"

### Week 1-2: Physical Examination Foundations
- **Talley Ch 1-5:** General principles, vital signs, hands, general examination
  - `Clinical_Examination_Split/part001-002` (pages 1-100)

  - `AMC Handbook of Clinical Assessment.pdf` pages 1-45

- **Talley Ch 6-14:** Cardiovascular, respiratory, GI, neurological systems
  - `Clinical_Examination_Split/part002-005` (pages 51-250)

  - `Murtagh_GP_part002, 006, 012` (selected chapters)

- **Oxford EM Ch 3-4, 8, 18:** Anaphylaxis, cardiac emergencies, GI emergencies, neurology
  - `Oxford_EM_part001-003` (pages 1-300)

  - `AMC Handbook of Clinical Assessment.pdf` pages 67-156

## Australian Protocol Verification

**IMPORTANT:** Some textbooks (Oxford EM, Churchill's) are UK-based. Always verify Australian protocols:

| Clinical Area | Australian Resource | Location |
|---------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Resuscitation** | ANZCOR Guidelines 2024 | External (www.resus.org.au) |
| **Drug Therapy** | eTG 2024 (Therapeutic Guidelines) | External (tg.org.au) |
| **PBS Medications** | PBS 2024 | External (pbs.gov.au) |
| **Antenatal Care** | KEMH Guidelines | `Antenatal guidelines kemh.pdf` |
| **General Practice** | Murtagh GP 8th Ed | ✅ Australian (already in Books/) |

**When in doubt:** Prioritize Murtagh GP and AMC resources over UK textbooks.
