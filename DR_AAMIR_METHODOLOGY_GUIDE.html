1Ô∏è‚É£ Dr. Aamir's 9 Core Principles

Principle 1: Differential-Driven Thinking

Start with differentials FROM THE FIRST QUESTION

  • Don't wait until the end to think about diagnosis
  • Opening question generates immediate differential list
  • Pattern recognition: Age + Symptom + Location = Differential List
  • Each question narrows or expands the differential
Example:

"58-year-old with central chest pain radiating to arm"

‚Üí Immediate thought: ACS (urgent), PE (urgent), GORD (common)

Next questions driven by: Ruling out ACS and PE FIRST

Principle 2: Structured Frameworks (Memorizable)

Use specific, learnable frameworks for every scenario

  • SOCRATES for pain assessment
  • 5 Ps for physical examination (Preparation, Position, Permission, Perform, Present)
  • SPIKES for breaking bad news
  • HIPJAP for cardiovascular exam (Hands, Inspection, Pulses, JVP, Apex, Praecordium)
  • IPTAP for respiratory exam (Inspection, Palpation, Tactile fremitus, Auscultation, Percussion)

Principle 3: Time-Conscious Station Design

Every station designed for EXACTLY 8 minutes

  • Clear time allocation for each section
  • Example: Opening 30 sec, HPC 3-4 min, Summary 1 min
  • Realistic timing that can be practiced and measured
  • Candidates know what to prioritize when time runs out

Principle 4: Australian Medical Context (CRITICAL)

100% Australian terminology, drugs, and guidelines

Australian Spelling:

  • ‚úÖ paediatric, anaemia, oesophagus, colour, haemoglobin
  • ‚ùå pediatric, anemia, esophagus, color, hemoglobin

Australian Drug Names:

  • ‚úÖ paracetamol, salbutamol, GTN, lignocaine
  • ‚ùå acetaminophen, albuterol, nitroglycerin, lidocaine

Australian Terminology:

  • ‚úÖ GP, Emergency Department, specialist, theatre, cannula
  • ‚ùå PCP, ER, attending, OR, IV line

Australian Guidelines:

  • Reference eTG 2024 for management
  • Reference AMH for medications
  • Include Medicare/PBS context (bulk-billed vs out-of-pocket)
  • Mention costs where relevant

Principle 5: IMG-Focused Teaching

"Common IMG Mistakes ‚ùå" section in EVERY document

  • Explicitly states what NOT to do
  • Anticipates errors from international training
  • Corrects US/UK/other country terminology confusion
  • Highlights examination technique differences

Principle 6: Word-for-Word Scripts

Exact phrases examiners expect to hear

  • Not just concepts - actual sentences to memorize
  • Example: "I can see this is very difficult news" (NOT "This must be hard")
  • Removes ambiguity about what to say
  • Communication stations have complete verbatim scripts

Principle 7: Top 3 Differentials (Always Ranked)

Never just list differentials - ALWAYS rank them

Ranking Criteria:

  1. Urgency - Life-threatening conditions first
  2. Likelihood - Most probable given clinical features
  3. "Don't miss" diagnoses - e.g., ectopic pregnancy in female with abdominal pain

Format:

1. [MOST URGENT/LIKELY] - Why this is #1
   Features: [Key features]
   Risk factors: [Present]
   Urgency: IMMEDIATE/URGENT/ROUTINE

2. [SECOND DIFFERENTIAL] - Why this is #2
   [Same format]

3. [THIRD DIFFERENTIAL] - Why this is #3
   [Same format]

Principle 8: Safety-Netting (Specific Red Flags)

NEVER vague - always specific red flags with actions

‚ùå Bad Safety-Netting:

  • "Come back if you're worse"
  • "Return if you have concerns"
  • "See your GP if it doesn't improve"

‚úÖ Good Safety-Netting (Dr. Aamir Style):

"Call 000 immediately if you develop:
- Severe chest pain
- Shortness of breath at rest
- Feel faint or collapse
- Pain spreading to your arm or jaw

Return to Emergency Department within 24 hours if:
- Pain is getting worse despite medication
- You develop sweating or nausea
- You have any concerns at all"

Principle 9: Examiner-Focused Presentation

Final summary directed at examiner, not patient

Structured Format:

"This is a [age]-year-old [gender] presenting with [complaint].

Key features include: [Feature 1], [Feature 2], [Feature 3]

My top 3 differentials are:
1. [Diagnosis 1] - [Brief justification]
2. [Diagnosis 2] - [Brief justification]
3. [Diagnosis 3] - [Brief justification]

My management plan would be:
- [Investigation 1]
- [Investigation 2]
- [Treatment approach]
- [Safety-netting]"

3Ô∏è‚É£ 8-Step Conversion Process

Use this process for EVERY extraction from textbooks:

Step 1: Read Textbook Section

  • Identify core clinical content
  • Note key diagnostic features
  • List treatment recommendations
  • Identify red flags/complications
  • Extract examination techniques (if physical exam)

Step 2: Generate Differential List

  • From textbook differentials, create ranked list
  • Order by: 1) Life-threatening first, 2) Most common, 3) Don't miss
  • Focus on Top 3 (maximum 5)
  • Add urgency tags: IMMEDIATE, URGENT, ROUTINE
  • Note distinguishing features for each

Step 3: Create Opening Statement (30 seconds)

  • Write standard introduction script
  • Add "Immediate Differential Thinking" box
  • Format: "Age/Gender with [complaint] ‚Üí Think: [Diff 1], [Diff 2], [Diff 3]"
  • State what the questions are targeting

Step 4: Build Systematic Framework

  • Choose appropriate framework:
    • Pain ‚Üí SOCRATES
    • Physical exam ‚Üí 5 Ps
    • Breaking bad news ‚Üí SPIKES
    • Other history ‚Üí Systematic approach
  • For each framework element, add "Differential Thinking" subsection
  • Link findings to specific differentials
  • Include exact questions to ask

Step 5: Australianize Content

  • Replace all US drug names:
    • acetaminophen ‚Üí paracetamol
    • albuterol ‚Üí salbutamol
    • nitroglycerin ‚Üí GTN
    • lidocaine ‚Üí lignocaine
    • epinephrine ‚Üí adrenaline
  • Change to Australian spelling:
    • Use -ae endings (paediatric, anaemia, haemoglobin)
    • Use -oe endings (oesophagus, oestrogen)
    • Use -our endings (colour, behaviour, tumour)
  • Add eTG 2024 guideline references
  • Note Medicare/PBS status:
    • "ECG - Medicare bulk-billed"
    • "MRI - not bulk-billed, ~$300-500 out-of-pocket"
  • Add bulk-billing context
  • Change to Australian healthcare terminology

Step 6: Add IMG Mistakes Section

  • Anticipate errors from US/UK/other training
  • List common terminology confusion:
    • ‚ùå "PCP" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "GP"
    • ‚ùå "ER" ‚Üí ‚úÖ "Emergency Department"
  • Add examination technique errors (if physical exam)
  • Include communication pitfalls
  • Minimum 5-8 mistakes listed
  • Format: "‚ùå [Wrong] - ‚úÖ [Correct]"

Step 7: Create Specific Safety-Netting

  • From textbook complications/red flags, create actionable advice
  • Format with two levels:
    • "Call 000 if" - Immediate life-threatening
    • "Return to ED if" - Urgent but not immediate
  • Be specific with symptoms (not vague)
  • Include timeframes where relevant
  • Add "You have any concerns at all - it's better to be checked"

Step 8: Write Examiner Summary

  • Create model summary statement
  • Include structured format:
    • Patient presentation
    • Key features
    • Top 3 differentials with brief justification
    • Management plan
  • Add clinical reasoning demonstration
  • Show observable criteria

Example 1: History-Taking Transformation

‚ùå BEFORE (Textbook Style - Talley & O'Connor):

"Chest pain is a common presenting symptom in medical practice. The differential diagnosis is broad and includes acute coronary syndrome, pulmonary embolism, pneumothorax, pericarditis, aortic dissection, pneumonia, and gastro-oesophageal reflux disease.

History should include detailed characterization of the pain including its location, radiation, quality, severity, timing, and associated symptoms. Risk factors for cardiac disease should be assessed. Physical examination should assess hemodynamic stability.

Appropriate investigations include electrocardiogram, cardiac biomarkers, chest radiograph, and further imaging as indicated."

‚úÖ AFTER (Dr. Aamir Style):

CHEST PAIN - History Taking

Opening Statement (30 seconds)

Immediate Differential Thinking:
‚Ä¢ Adult with chest pain ‚Üí Think immediately: ACS (life-threatening), PE (life-threatening), GORD (common but benign)
‚Ä¢ Your questions are driven by ruling out ACS and PE FIRST
‚Ä¢ Everything you ask should narrow these differentials

"Good morning, I'm Dr [Name]. I understand you've come in today with chest pain. I'd like to ask you some questions about your symptoms to better understand what might be causing this. Is that alright with you?"

Focused History - SOCRATES Framework (3-4 minutes)

S - Site: "Can you point to where exactly you feel the pain?"

Differential Thinking by Location:
‚Ä¢ Central/left-sided ‚Üí Think: ACS, PE, pericarditis, aortic dissection
‚Ä¢ Right-sided ‚Üí Think: PE, pneumothorax, musculoskeletal
‚Ä¢ Epigastric ‚Üí Think: GORD, peptic ulcer, inferior MI (don't miss!)

O - Onset: "When did the pain start? What were you doing when it started? Did it come on suddenly or gradually?"

Differential Thinking by Onset:
‚Ä¢ Sudden (seconds) ‚Üí URGENT: Aortic dissection, PE, pneumothorax
‚Ä¢ Gradual (minutes-hours) ‚Üí ACS, pericarditis, pneumonia
‚Ä¢ Chronic/recurrent ‚Üí GORD, stable angina

[Continue with complete SOCRATES...]

Top 3 Differentials (Ranked by Urgency)

1. Acute Coronary Syndrome (ACS) - Most concerning, requires urgent exclusion
‚Ä¢ Features: Central/left-sided crushing or tight chest pain, radiation to left arm/jaw/neck
‚Ä¢ Associated symptoms: Sweating, nausea, dyspnoea, pallor
‚Ä¢ Risk factors: Age >45 (male) or >55 (female), smoking, diabetes, hypertension, hyperlipidaemia, family history of IHD
‚Ä¢ Urgency: IMMEDIATE - call for senior help, do not leave patient

2. Pulmonary Embolism (PE) - Life-threatening, needs urgent assessment
‚Ä¢ Features: Sharp, pleuritic chest pain (worse on deep breathing/coughing)
‚Ä¢ Associated symptoms: Dyspnoea, haemoptysis, tachycardia, hypoxia, unilateral leg swelling
‚Ä¢ Risk factors: Recent surgery/immobility, long-haul flight (>4 hours), cancer, OCP, pregnancy, previous VTE, thrombophilia
‚Ä¢ Urgency: URGENT - needs CTPA and anticoagulation

3. Gastro-oesophageal Reflux Disease (GORD) - Most common cause, benign
‚Ä¢ Features: Burning retrosternal pain, sour taste in mouth, belching
‚Ä¢ Timing: Related to meals, worse lying flat or bending over
‚Ä¢ Relieved by: Antacids (Quick-Eze, Mylanta), sitting upright
‚Ä¢ Risk factors: Obesity, hiatus hernia, certain foods (spicy, fatty, caffeine)

Investigations (Australian Context)

Immediate (Medicare bulk-billed):
‚Ä¢ 12-lead ECG - MUST be done within 10 minutes for suspected ACS. Look for: ST elevation (STEMI), ST depression/T-wave inversion (NSTEMI), Q waves (old MI)
‚Ä¢ Troponin - 0 hours and 3 hours (serial troponins mandatory for ACS rule-out)
‚Ä¢ Chest X-ray - pneumonia, pneumothorax, widened mediastinum (aortic dissection), cardiomegaly
‚Ä¢ Bloods: FBC, UEC, CRP, lipid profile, D-dimer (if PE suspected)

Further investigations if indicated:
‚Ä¢ CT pulmonary angiogram (CTPA) - if moderate-high suspicion of PE (not bulk-billed in private, ~$300-500 out-of-pocket; free in public ED)
‚Ä¢ Coronary angiography - if ACS confirmed, for PCI

Management (Therapeutic Guidelines eTG 2024)

If ACS suspected - IMMEDIATE ACTION:
1. Call for senior help immediately
2. Nil by mouth, IV access, continuous ECG monitoring
3. MONA protocol:
   ‚Ä¢ Morphine 2.5-5mg IV (if severe pain)
   ‚Ä¢ Oxygen only if SpO‚ÇÇ <94% (not routinely)
   ‚Ä¢ Nitrates: GTN 300-600mcg sublingual (up to 3 doses)
   ‚Ä¢ Aspirin 300mg PO chewed
4. Additional medications:
   ‚Ä¢ Clopidogrel 300mg loading dose (if STEMI)
   ‚Ä¢ Atorvastatin 80mg

If PE likely:
‚Ä¢ Therapeutic anticoagulation: Enoxaparin 1mg/kg SC BD (if haemodynamically stable)
‚Ä¢ Arrange urgent CTPA
‚Ä¢ Admit for observation

If GORD suspected:
‚Ä¢ Lifestyle modifications: Weight loss, avoid trigger foods, elevate head of bed
‚Ä¢ Proton pump inhibitor (PPI): Esomeprazole 40mg daily (PBS-listed)
‚Ä¢ PRN antacids: Quick-Eze or Mylanta (available over-the-counter)

Safety-Netting (Specific Red Flags)

"I'm going to give you some specific warning signs. If you experience ANY of these, please call 000 or come back to the Emergency Department immediately:

Call 000 if:
‚Ä¢ The chest pain becomes severe or feels crushing/tight
‚Ä¢ You develop shortness of breath at rest
‚Ä¢ You feel faint, dizzy, or collapse
‚Ä¢ The pain spreads to your arm, jaw, back, or neck
‚Ä¢ You start sweating heavily or vomiting
‚Ä¢ You feel like something is seriously wrong

Return to Emergency Department within 24 hours if:
‚Ä¢ Pain is getting worse despite medication
‚Ä¢ You develop fever or cough with chest pain
‚Ä¢ You have any concerns at all - it's better to be checked"

Common IMG Mistakes ‚ùå

1. ‚ùå Not asking about radiation to jaw/arm - Critical for ACS diagnosis, commonly missed
2. ‚ùå Using "nitroglycerin" - Say "GTN" (glyceryl trinitrate) in Australia
3. ‚ùå Saying "ER" - Australian term is "Emergency Department" or "ED"
4. ‚ùå Not mentioning Medicare bulk-billing status - Patients want to know if investigations are free
5. ‚ùå Vague safety-netting - Don't just say "come back if worse" - be specific about red flags
6. ‚ùå Not checking for risk factors systematically - Must ask about ALL cardiac risk factors
7. ‚ùå Forgetting to ask about previous cardiac history - Previous MI/PCI/CABG is critical
8. ‚ùå Not ranking differentials - Always state "My top 3 differentials are..." with #1, #2, #3

6Ô∏è‚É£ Common Mistakes to Avoid

When Creating OSCE Notes, NEVER:

  • ‚ùå Use US drug names (acetaminophen, albuterol, nitroglycerin)
  • ‚ùå Use US spelling (pediatric, anemia, color)
  • ‚ùå Use US terminology (ER, PCP, attending)
  • ‚ùå Forget "Common IMG Mistakes" section
  • ‚ùå Give vague safety-netting ("come back if worse")
  • ‚ùå List differentials without ranking them
  • ‚ùå Omit time allocations
  • ‚ùå Forget to cite eTG 2024 for management
  • ‚ùå Skip Medicare/PBS context for investigations
  • ‚ùå Write generic scripts - be specific with exact phrases
  • ‚ùå Omit differential thinking cues in opening
  • ‚ùå Create stations longer than 8 minutes
