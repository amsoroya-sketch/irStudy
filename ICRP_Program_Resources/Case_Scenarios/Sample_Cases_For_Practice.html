Case 1: Chest Pain - Cardiac

Patient Presentation

Name: John Smith Age: 58 years Sex: Male Presenting Complaint: "I have severe chest pain"

If Asked (History Taking Practice):

HPC (History of Presenting Complaint):
  • Onset: Started 2 hours ago while mowing the lawn
  • Site: Central chest
  • Character: Heavy, crushing, "like an elephant sitting on my chest"
  • Radiation: Radiates to left arm and jaw
  • Severity: 9/10
  • Duration: Constant since onset
  • Associated symptoms: Sweating profusely, nausea, short of breath
  • Relieving factors: Nothing helps, tried resting
  • Exacerbating factors: Any movement makes it worse

PMH:
  • Hypertension (10 years)
  • Type 2 Diabetes (5 years)
  • High cholesterol

Medications:
  • Metformin 1000mg BD
  • Amlodipine 10mg daily
  • Atorvastatin 40mg nocte

Allergies: None known FH:
  • Father had heart attack at age 60
  • Mother has diabetes

SH:
  • Smokes 20 cigarettes/day for 30 years (30 pack-years)
  • Occasional alcohol (2-3 beers on weekends)
  • Works as accountant (sedentary)
  • Lives with wife
  • No regular exercise

Physical Examination Findings:

Vitals:
  • BP: 160/95 mmHg
  • HR: 110 bpm, regular
  • RR: 22/min
  • Temp: 36.8¬∞C
  • O‚ÇÇ Sat: 94% on room air

General: Distressed, sweating, clutching chest CVS: Heart sounds normal, no murmurs. Peripheral pulses present. Respiratory: Clear breath sounds bilaterally Other: NAD

  • MONA protocol: Morphine, Oxygen, Nitrates, Aspirin

Case 2: Shortness of Breath - Respiratory

Patient Presentation

Name: Mary Johnson Age: 72 years Sex: Female Presenting Complaint: "I can't breathe properly"

If Asked:

HPC:
  • Onset: Worsening over 3 days
  • Character: Feels like can't get enough air
  • Associated: Productive cough with green sputum, fever, chills
  • Severity: Can only speak in short sentences
  • Exacerbating: Worse when lying flat
  • Relieving: Sitting up helps slightly
  • No chest pain

PMH:
  • COPD (diagnosed 5 years ago)
  • Previous pneumonia 2 years ago

Medications:
  • Salbutamol inhaler PRN
  • Tiotropium inhaler daily
  • Doesn't use inhaled steroids (ran out)

Allergies: Penicillin (rash) SH:
  • Ex-smoker (quit 5 years ago, 40 pack-year history)
  • Widowed, lives alone
  • Daughter visits daily
  • Uses walking frame at home

Physical Examination Findings:

Vitals:
  • BP: 135/80 mmHg
  • HR: 105 bpm
  • RR: 28/min
  • Temp: 38.5¬∞C
  • O‚ÇÇ Sat: 88% on room air

General: Dyspnoeic, using accessory muscles Respiratory:
  • Reduced air entry right base
  • Coarse crackles right lower zone
  • Dull to percussion right base
  • Tactile vocal fremitus increased right base

CVS: Tachycardic, regular, HS normal Other: NAD

  • Oxygen - Target 88-92% in COPD patient
