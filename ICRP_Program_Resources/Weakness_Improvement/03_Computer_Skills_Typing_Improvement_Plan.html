Why These Skills Matter for ICRP

Daily ICRP Reality

During your 12-week ICRP program at Young Hospital, you will:

  • See 5-15 patients per day
  • Document every patient encounter in EMR (Electronic Medical Records)
  • Type clinical notes including history, examination findings, assessment, and plan
  • Enter orders for investigations and medications (with supervisor countersignature)
  • Navigate multiple EMR screens - patient demographics, medication lists, investigation results, etc.
  • Respond to time pressure - ward rounds move fast, notes must be completed promptly
  • Be observed by supervisors - they will notice if you're struggling with computers

Week 1 Critical Rules:

Week 6: Clinical Note Typing

Goals:

  • Type complete clinical notes
  • Practice SOAP format
  • Reach 35+ WPM

SOAP Note Format Practice:

S - Subjective (Patient's story)

Example template to type repeatedly:

55-year-old male presenting with chest pain x 3 days. Pain is central, crushing in nature, radiates to left arm. Associated SOB and sweating. Pain worse on exertion, relieved by rest. No previous episodes.

O - Objective (Your findings)

O/E: Patient appears uncomfortable. HR 95 regular, BP 145/92, RR 18, Sats 96% RA. CVS: HS I+II+0, no murmurs. Chest clear. Abdomen soft, non-tender.

A - Assessment (Your diagnosis)

Likely acute coronary syndrome vs angina. Differential includes GERD, musculoskeletal pain.

P - Plan (What next)

ECG, troponin, FBC, electrolytes. Aspirin 300mg stat given. Discussed with Dr Smith. Plan for admission to CCU for observation and further investigation.

Daily Practice:

During ICRP: Maintaining & Applying Skills

First Week Strategy

  • Observe First: Watch how experienced staff use the EMR system
  • Ask Questions: "Can you show me how to navigate to...?"
  • Take Notes: Write down common workflows and shortcuts
  • Practice After Hours: If possible, practice navigating EMR (with dummy patients if available)
  • Document Promptly: Type notes ASAP while memory fresh

EMR Documentation Tips for ICRP

  1. Prepare Before Typing:
    • Review your handwritten notes
    • Know what you're going to write
    • Structure in your head first
  2. Use Templates:
    • Most EMRs have note templates - USE THEM
    • Modify existing templates
    • Create your own templates if allowed
  3. Type Efficiently:
    • Use abbreviations (but spell out important terms)
    • Copy-paste appropriately (update details!)
    • Use auto-text/shortcuts if available
  4. Save Frequently:
    • Ctrl+S every few minutes
    • Don't lose your work!
  5. Review Before Finalizing:
    • Spell check
    • Read through for errors
    • Ensure all sections complete
