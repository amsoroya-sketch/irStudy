4. OSCE Stations - Complete Guide

üéØ Accessing OSCE Stations

  1. Open: ICRP_OSCE_Preparation/Mock_Stations/00_MASTER_INDEX_MOCK_OSCE_STATIONS.html
  2. Browse by color-coded specialty cards (Medicine=Blue, Surgery=Red, ObGyn=Pink, Mixed=Purple)
  3. Click any station to open

üìã Station Components (What's in Each Station)

Component Purpose
1. Candidate Instructions What YOU read before entering the station (your task)
2. Patient Script Exact words the simulated patient will say (for partner practice)
3. Examiner Instructions What examiners look for, critical failures, pass/fail criteria
4. Marking Checklist 80-point rubric (pass = 56/80 = 70%)
5. Model Answer Perfect response example with full history/management
6. IMG Common Mistakes Top 10 errors IMGs make + how to avoid them

üé≠ Practice Methods

Method 1: Solo Practice

  1. Read candidate instructions (1 minute)
  2. Set timer: 8 minutes
  3. Record yourself OR write notes of what you'd say/do
  4. Review: Compare with model answer
  5. Self-assess: Use marking checklist (aim for ‚â•56/80)
  6. Review: IMG mistakes section - did you make any?

Method 2: Partner Practice (Recommended)

  1. Partner A: Candidate (reads candidate instructions, timed 8 mins)
  2. Partner B: Simulated Patient (uses patient script)
  3. After 8 mins: Partner B assesses using marking checklist
  4. Feedback: Discuss performance, review IMG mistakes together
  5. Swap roles: Repeat with different station

Method 3: Group Practice (3-4 People)

  1. Person 1: Candidate (timed 8 mins)
  2. Person 2: Simulated Patient (uses script)
  3. Person 3: Examiner (uses marking checklist)
  4. Person 4: Observer (takes notes)
  5. After 8 mins: Group feedback (5 mins)
  6. Rotate roles: Each person practices each station

üìã Key Frameworks & Mnemonics

5 Ps Framework (Dyspnoea)

  • P1 - Precipitant: What triggers breathlessness?
  • P2 - Progression: Getting better or worse?
  • P3 - Pattern: Constant vs intermittent?
  • P4 - Positional: Orthopnoea? Paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnoea?
  • P5 - Previous: Previous episodes or similar symptoms?

5 Ps Framework (Syncope)

  • P1 - Precipitant: What were you doing?
  • P2 - Posture: Standing, sitting, lying?
  • P3 - Phenomena: Warning? Aura? Palpitations?
  • P4 - Post-ictal: Confusion after? Tongue biting? Incontinence?
  • P5 - Previous: Ever happened before?

SOCRATES (Pain Assessment)

  • S - Site: Where is the pain?
  • O - Onset: When did it start? Sudden or gradual?
  • C - Character: Sharp, dull, burning, crushing?
  • R - Radiation: Does it spread anywhere?
  • A - Associated symptoms: Nausea, vomiting, fever?
  • T - Timing: Constant or intermittent? How long?
  • E - Exacerbating/relieving: What makes it better/worse?
  • S - Severity: 0-10 scale? Impact on daily life?

CAM Criteria (Delirium Diagnosis)

ALL 4 must be present for delirium diagnosis:

  1. Acute onset & fluctuating course - Sudden change, waxing/waning
  2. Inattention - Cannot focus, easily distracted
  3. Disorganized thinking - Incoherent, illogical conversation
  4. Altered consciousness - Hyperalert OR drowsy

The Big 8 (Falls Assessment)

Must assess ALL 8 categories systematically:

  1. Previous falls - ‚â•2 falls/year = high risk
  2. Medications - Polypharmacy (‚â•5) + fall-risk drugs
  3. Vision - Cataracts, glasses outdated
  4. Gait & Balance - Unsteady, uses aid, reduced mobility
  5. Cardiovascular - Postural hypotension, syncope
  6. Neurological - Stroke, Parkinson's, neuropathy
  7. Home Environment - Rugs, lighting, rails, footwear
  8. Fear of Falling - Reduced activity ‚Üí deconditioning

PINCH ME (Delirium Causes)

  • P - Pain
  • I - Infection (UTI, pneumonia)
  • N - Nutrition (dehydration, malnutrition)
  • C - Constipation
  • H - Hypoxia
  • M - Medications (anticholinergics, benzodiazepines)
  • E - Environment (new setting, sensory deprivation)
